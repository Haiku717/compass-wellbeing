Privacy & Cookies Policy

How we collect, use and protect your personal information.

1. Introduction

Compass Wellbeing is committed to protecting the privacy of our website visitors. This policy applies where we are acting as the data controller for the personal data of our website visitors; in other words, where we determine the purposes and means of the processing of that personal data. We use cookies on our website. Insofar as those cookies are not strictly necessary for the provision of our website, we will ask you to consent to our use of cookies when you first visit.

2. How we use your personal data

In this section we set out the general categories of personal data that we may process, and the purposes for which we may process that data.

  • Usage data: information about your use of the website, including your IP address, geographical location, browser type and version, operating system and navigation paths. This is collected via analytics and used to analyse and improve our website. The legal basis is our legitimate interests in monitoring and improving our services.
  • Account data: information you provide to us, used to operate the website, provide our services, ensure security, maintain backups and communicate with you. The legal basis is our legitimate interests in the proper administration of our website and business.
  • Enquiry data: information contained in any enquiry you submit regarding our services, processed for the purpose of responding to and managing your enquiry. The legal basis is your consent.
  • Transaction data: information relating to transactions, including purchases of services, processed to supply the services and keep proper records. The legal basis is the performance of a contract and our legitimate interests.
  • Notification data: information you provide for the purpose of subscribing to email notifications and newsletters, processed to send you those communications. The legal basis is your consent.

We may also process any of your personal data where necessary for the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims; for obtaining or maintaining insurance, managing risks, or obtaining professional advice; or where processing is necessary to protect your vital interests or those of another person.

Please do not supply any other person’s personal data to us unless we prompt you to do so.

3. Providing your personal data to others

We may disclose your personal data to our insurers and/or professional advisers, and to our suppliers or subcontractors (such as cloud, hosting, backup and communications providers) insofar as reasonably necessary for the purposes set out in this policy. We may also disclose personal data where required for legal compliance, to protect vital interests, or to establish, exercise or defend legal claims.

4. International transfers of your personal data

Personal data may be stored and processed by our service providers in countries including Australia, the Philippines, Singapore, Ukraine, the United Arab Emirates and the United States for the purposes of hosting, backup and content delivery. We take appropriate safeguards for such transfers, such as standard contractual clauses.

You acknowledge that personal data you submit for publication through our website may be available, via the internet, around the world. We cannot prevent the use (or misuse) of such personal data by others.

5. Retaining and deleting personal data

Personal data that we process for any purpose shall not be kept for longer than is necessary for that purpose. We will retain your personal data where such retention is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation to which we are subject, or in order to protect your vital interests or the vital interests of another person.

6. Security of personal data

We will take appropriate technical and organisational precautions to secure your personal data and to prevent its loss, misuse or alteration. Data relating to financial transactions is encrypted using appropriate technology. You acknowledge that the transmission of unencrypted data over the internet is inherently insecure, and you are responsible for keeping any password you use confidential.

7. Amendments

We may update this policy from time to time by publishing a new version on our website. You should check this page occasionally to ensure you are happy with any changes.

8. Your rights

Under data protection law you have a number of rights in relation to your personal data, including the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, objection, and data portability, as well as the right to complain to a supervisory authority and to withdraw consent.

Right to access

You have the right to confirmation as to whether or not we process your personal data and, where we do, access to that personal data together with certain additional information. The first copy is provided free of charge; additional copies may be subject to a reasonable fee.

Right to rectification

You have the right to have any inaccurate personal data about you corrected and, taking into account the purposes of the processing, to have any incomplete personal data completed.

Right to erasure

In certain circumstances you have the right to have your personal data erased without undue delay, for example where the data is no longer necessary, you withdraw consent, or the processing is unlawful. Exclusions apply, such as where processing is necessary for freedom of expression, compliance with a legal obligation, or legal claims.

Right to restrict processing

In certain circumstances you have the right to restrict the processing of your personal data. Where processing has been restricted, we may continue to store your data, but will only otherwise process it with your consent, for legal claims, to protect the rights of others, or for reasons of important public interest.

Right to object

You may object to our processing of your personal data on grounds relating to your particular situation, where the legal basis is our legitimate interests. We will stop processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds, or the processing is for legal claims. You may object at any time to processing for direct marketing purposes, after which we will stop.

Right to data portability

Where the legal basis is consent or contract, and processing is carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format. This right does not apply where it would adversely affect the rights and freedoms of others.

Right to complain and to withdraw consent

You may lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. Where the legal basis for processing is your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time; withdrawal will not affect the lawfulness of processing before the withdrawal. You may exercise your rights by written notice to us.

10. Updating information

Please let us know if the personal information that we hold about you needs to be corrected or updated.

11. About cookies

A cookie is a file containing an identifier (a string of letters and numbers) that is sent by a web server to a web browser and is stored by the browser. The identifier is then sent back to the server each time the browser requests a page from the server. Cookies may be either persistent (remaining until their expiry date, unless deleted earlier) or session cookies (expiring at the end of the browser session). Cookies do not typically contain any information that personally identifies a user, but personal information that we store about you may be linked to information stored in cookies.

12. Cookies that we use

We use cookies for the following purposes: recognising your computer when you visit; storing your cookie consent preferences; and website analysis. We also use local storage for functions such as browser tab synchronisation and saving your settings.

13. Cookies used by our service providers

Our service providers use cookies which may be stored on your computer when you visit our website. We use Google Analytics to analyse the use of our website; it gathers information about website use by means of cookies. We also use Cloudflare to help identify malicious visitors, reduce the chance of blocking legitimate users, and provide customised services.

14. Managing cookies

Most browsers allow you to refuse to accept cookies and to delete cookies. The methods for doing so vary from browser to browser, and from version to version. Blocking all cookies will have a negative impact upon the usability of many websites. If you block cookies, you may not be able to use all the features on our website.
